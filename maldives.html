TOURIST PLACES OF INDIA

MALDIVES OF INDIA




The Maldives is a tropical island nation in the Indian Ocean, southwest of India and Sri Lanka.

It consists of 26 atolls made up of around 1,200 coral islands, of which about 200 are inhabited.

The capital city of the Maldives is Malé.
Known for its white sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant coral reefs.

It is the lowest country in the world, with an average ground level of just 1.5 meters above sea level.

The Maldives has a tropical climate with warm temperatures and high humidity year-round.

It is a popular luxury travel destination, famous for overwater villas and private island resorts.

The Maldives is one of the best places in the world for snorkeling and scuba diving, especially in coral reefs.

The official religion is Islam, and the culture is a blend of South Indian, Sinhalese, and Arab influences.

The currency of Maldives is the Maldivian Rufiyaa (MVR).

The main economic sectors are tourism, fishing, and boat building.

The Maldivian language is Dhivehi, which has roots in Sanskrit, Arabic, and Sinhalese.

Transportation between islands is mainly by speedboat, seaplane, or domestic flights.

Due to climate change and rising sea levels, the Maldives is at high risk of submergence.

It is ideal for honeymoons, destination weddings, and romantic getaways because of its serene and luxurious setting.
