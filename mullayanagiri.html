TOURIST PLACES OF INDIA

MULLYANAGIRI, CHICKMANGALORE




Mullayanagiri is the highest peak in Karnataka, with an elevation of 1,930 meters (6,330 feet) above sea level.

It is located in the Chikkamagaluru district in the Western Ghats of Karnataka.

The peak is part of the Baba Budangiri Range, also known as the Chandra Drona Hills.

It is a popular destination for trekking, nature walks, and adventure tourism.

The peak is named after a sage called "Mullappa Swamy", who is believed to have meditated in the caves near the summit.

A small temple dedicated to Mullappa Swamy is located at the top of the hill.

Mullayanagiri offers panoramic views of the surrounding valleys, coffee plantations, and lush greenery.

The region is known for its cool climate, especially pleasant during the early mornings and post-monsoon.

The trek to Mullayanagiri is relatively easy to moderate and popular among both beginners and seasoned trekkers.

The nearest town is Chikkamagaluru, located around 20 kilometers from the base of the hill.

The area is rich in flora and fauna, making it a great place for nature lovers and bird watchers.

The roads leading to the top are narrow and winding, offering a thrilling bike ride experience.

Sunrise and sunset views from the peak are stunning and attract many photographers and travelers.

Mullayanagiri is part of the Bhadra Wildlife Sanctuary zone, contributing to the region’s ecological importance.

It is one of the most visited hill stations in South India, especially popular among weekend travelers from Bengaluru and Mangalore.
