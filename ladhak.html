TOURIST PLACES OF INDIA

LADAKH, JAMMU AND KASHMIR




Ladakh is a Union Territory of India, created in October 2019 after the bifurcation of Jammu and Kashmir.

It is known as the "Land of High Passes" due to its rugged mountainous terrain.

Ladakh shares international borders with China (Tibet) and Pakistan, making it strategically important.

The two main districts are Leh and Kargil.

Ladakh is famous for its Buddhist culture, monasteries (like Hemis, Thiksey, Diskit), and festivals.

The region is home to the highest motorable road in the world, Khardung La Pass.

Major rivers include the Indus River, which originates here, and its tributaries.

Ladakh's population is mainly Buddhist and Shia Muslim.

Srinagar is the summer capital, and Jammu is the winter capital of the UT.

Known for its natural beauty, with Dal Lake, Gulmarg, Pahalgam, Sonmarg as top attractions.

Kashmir is famous for houseboats, shikaras, apple orchards, and saffron fields.

Major religions include Islam (majority in Kashmir) and Hinduism (majority in Jammu).
